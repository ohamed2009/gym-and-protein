Spaghetti Bolognese

Classic Italian Pasta Dish - Easy & Delicious

⏱️ Prep Time 15 minutes
🍳 Cook Time 30 minutes
👥 Servings 4 servings
📊 Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti pasta
  • 500g ground beef (80/20 blend)
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 400g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 100ml whole milk
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Parmesan cheese for topping
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional garnish)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Dice the onion and mince the garlic. Set aside. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil.
  2. Brown the meat: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until fully browned (about 5-7 minutes). Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Sauté aromatics: Add diced onion to the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste: Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes to caramelize slightly, which enhances the flavor.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes: Pour in the canned crushed tomatoes. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  6. Season the sauce: Add bay leaf, oregano, basil, salt, and black pepper. Stir and bring to a simmer.
  7. Simmer the sauce: Reduce heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken and flavors will develop.
  8. Add milk: In the last 5 minutes of cooking, slowly add milk while stirring. This creates a creamy, authentic Bolognese sauce. Do not boil.
  9. Cook the pasta: While the sauce simmers, add spaghetti to the boiling water. Cook according to package directions (typically 8-12 minutes) until al dente.
  10. Combine and serve: Drain the pasta and divide into serving bowls. Top generously with the Bolognese sauce. Sprinkle with fresh parmesan cheese and basil if desired.

Chef's Tips & Variations

  • Make it vegetarian: Replace ground beef with finely chopped mushrooms and lentils for a plant-based version.
  • Cook ahead: This sauce freezes well for up to 3 months. Make a double batch and freeze portions.
  • Wine addition: Add 100ml of red wine after browning the meat for deeper flavor (let it simmer off before adding tomatoes).
  • Fresh vs. canned tomatoes: In summer, use fresh tomatoes (about 6-8 medium ones, blended). In winter, canned tomatoes are just as good.
  • Pasta choice: While spaghetti is traditional, this sauce works beautifully with pappardelle, tagliatelle, or rigatoni.
